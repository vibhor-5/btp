PyTorch Time-Series Transformers

The Problem: Censored Demand

In perishable retail, observable sales data is artificially truncated when an item goes out of stock.

  • Missing Not At Random (MNAR): Zero sales doesn't mean zero demand; it means zero inventory.
  • Training forecasting models on censored sales leads to systemic underestimation.
  • This causes a vicious cycle of stockouts and lost revenue.

Data Preprocessing

Feature Engineering

  • Numerical: Weather, discounts, historical sales.
  • Categorical: Store, city, product hierarchy, holidays.
  • Target: Binary (1 if hourly stockout count > 0).

Transformations

  • Missing values handled (0 for num, -1 for cat).
  • StandardScaler for numerical covariates.
  • LabelEncoder for categorical entities.

Time-Series Sliding Window

Structuring the data for sequence-to-sequence forecasting:

  • History (14 Days): Captures past sales, weather, and previous stockout occurrences.
  • Future (7 Days): Provides known future covariates (e.g., weather forecasts, upcoming holidays).
  • Critical Step: sale_amount is strictly removed from future covariates to prevent data leakage.

Architecture: Time-Series Transformer

  • Categorical Embeddings: High-dimensional representations of stores and products.
  • Positional Encodings: Preserving the sequential order of the 14-day history and 7-day future.
  • Encoder-Decoder: Encoder processes history, Decoder attends to history using future covariates.
